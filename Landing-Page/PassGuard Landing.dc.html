How your data moves
encrypted before it ever leaves your device →
Client-side
ZERO-KNOWLEDGE
RSA-OAEP · 2048-bit

Asymmetric key pairs generated via window.crypto.subtle secure every password shared between users.

PBKDF2 key derivation

A 256-bit AES-GCM key is derived from your master password with 100,000 iterations of HMAC-SHA-256 and a 16-byte random salt.

AES-GCM · 256-bit

Your private key is encrypted before it ever reaches the database — a compromised server still can't read it without your master password.

Server-side
DEFENCE IN DEPTH
AES-256-CBC encryption

Stored credential records are encrypted with Node's crypto module; a unique IV is appended to every cipher as iv:encryptedHex.

bcryptjs hashing

Login passwords are hashed with a salted work-factor — cleartext is never written to disk.

Layered by design

Client-side zero-knowledge and server-side encryption compound — neither layer trusts the other with plaintext.

The flow

From master password to autofill

Follow a single credential as it moves through PassGuard. Scroll to trace the path — the line fills as each stage completes.

01
PLAINTEXT · ON DEVICE
Your master password stays on your device

It is never transmitted. Everything sensitive is unlocked locally, in your browser — the server never sees it.

02
PBKDF2 → AES KEY
PBKDF2 derives your encryption key

100,000 iterations of HMAC-SHA-256 with a 16-byte random salt turn your password into a 256-bit AES-GCM key.

03
CIPHERTEXT
The vault is encrypted before it leaves

Credentials and your RSA private key are encrypted client-side, so what reaches our database is already ciphertext.

04
RSA KEY PAIR
RSA key pairs power secure sharing

A 2048-bit RSA-OAEP pair, generated with window.crypto.subtle, lets others receive access without decrypting your vault.

05
SEALED ENVELOPE
Access travels as an encrypted envelope

A shared secret is sealed to the recipient’s public key — only their private key can open it, and the plaintext is never exposed.

06
AUTOFILL · FROZEN
The extension fills, submits and freezes

On the recipient’s side, the extension autofills, auto-submits and freezes the window and controls so nothing can leak.
